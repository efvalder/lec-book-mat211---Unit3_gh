10  L27: Convolution Integral (8.6)

When applying the Laplace transform to solve differential equations, it is common to encounter situations where the right-hand side of the equation consists of the product of functions. For each of these functions, we may already know the corresponding inverse Laplace transform. This familiarity might lead us to incorrectly assume that the inverse Laplace transform of the product of functions is simply the product of their individual inverse transforms. However, this assumption is incorrect. The Laplace transform of a product of functions does not generally equal the product of their individual Laplace transforms. This misconception underscores the importance of applying the correct properties and methods when working with Laplace transforms in differential equations.

10.1 Inverse of the Product?

\[\boxed{\mathscr{L}^{-1}\{F(s)\,G(s)\}\neq \mathscr{L}^{-1}\{F(s)\}\mathscr{L}^{-1}\{G(s)\}}\]

10.2 Convolution Theorem

NoteTheorem

Theorem 10.1 Let \(f(t)\) and \(g(t)\) be continuous functions for \(t\geq 0\) and of exponential order

Let \(F(s)=\mathscr{L}\{f(t)\}\) and \(G(s)=\mathscr{L}\{g(t)\}\)

Then

\[\mathscr{L}^{-1}\{F(s)\,G(s)\}=\int_0^t f(z)g(t-z)\,dz\] \[=(f*g)(t)\]

CautionProof

\[F(s)\,G(s)=\int_0^\infty e^{-sz} f(z)\,dz\int_0^\infty e^{-s\tau} g(\tau)\,d\tau\]

\[=\int_0^\infty\left(\int_0^\infty e^{-s(\tau+z)} f(z)g(\tau)\,d\tau\right) dz\] Change of variables: \(t=\tau +z\rightarrow dt =d\tau\)

\[=\int_0^\infty\left(\int_{t=z}^\infty e^{-st}f(z)g(t-z)dt\right) dz\] Domain of integration:

Changing order of integration for the same region:

Warning in arrows(x, 0, x, x, length = 0.1, angle = 15, col = "red"):
zero-length arrow is of indeterminate angle and so skipped

\[=\int_0^\infty\left(\int_{z=0}^t e^{-st}f(z)g(t-z)dz \right)d\tau \]

\[=\int_0^\infty e^{-st}\left(\int_0^t f(z)g(t-z)dz \right)d\tau \]

\[=\mathscr{L}\left\{\int_0^t f(z)g(t-z)dz\right\}\]

\[=\mathscr{L}\left\{(f*g)(t)\right\}\]

\[\hspace{3in}\square\]

10.3 Example 1, Instead of partial fractions

TipExample

Calculate \[\mathscr{L}^{-1}\left\{\frac{1}{(s-2)(s-3)}\right\}\] w/o using partial fractions.

CautionSolution

\[\mathscr{L}^{-1}\left\{\frac{1}{(s-2)(s-3)}\right\}=\int_0^t f(z)g(t-z)\,dz\] \[=\int_0^t e^{2z}e^{3(t-z)}dz\]

\[=e^{3t}\int_0^t e^{-z}dz\]

\[=e^{3t} \left(\frac{e^{-z}}{-1}\Big|_0^t\right)\]

\[=-e^{3t} \left(e^{-t}-e^0\right)\] \[=e^{3t} \left(1-e^{-t}\right)\] \[=e^{3t} -e^{2t}\] checking with maxima-cas:

partfrac(1/((s-2)*(s-3)),s);

10.5 Property of convolution function

\[\mathscr{L}\{(f*g)(t)\}=\mathscr{L}\{f(t)\}\mathscr{L}\{g(t)\}\]

10.6 Example 1, solving IVP

TipExample

Suppose \(y=f(t)\) is the solution of the constant-coefficient homogeneous initial value problem \[y''+Py'+Qy=0,\qquad y(0)=0,\quad y'(0)=1\] Then the solution of the inhomogeneous initial value problem

\[y''+Py'+Qy=g(t),\qquad y(0)=0,\quad y'(0)=0\]

is \[y(t)=(f*g)(t)\]

CautionSolution

Taking the Laplace Transform of homogeneous eqn:

\[s^2Y-1+PsY+QY=0\] \[Y=\frac{1}{s^2+Ps+Q}\] \[y=f(t)=\mathscr{L}^{-1}\left\{\frac{1}{s^2+Ps+Q}\right\}\] Taking Laplace transform of non-homogeneous case

\[s^2Y+PsY+QY=G\] \[Y=\frac{G}{s^2+Ps+Q}\] \[y(t)=\mathscr{L}^{-1}\left\{\frac{G}{s^2+Ps+Q}\right\}\] \[=\mathscr{L}^{-1}\left\{G(s)F(s)\right\}\] \[=(f*g)(t)\] \[\hspace{3in}\square\]

10.7 Volterra Integral Equation

NoteDefinition

An equation of the form

\[y(t)=f(t)+ \int_0^t k(t-\tau)y(\tau)\,d\tau\]

is a Volterra integral equation. Here \(f\) and \(k\) are given functions and \(y\) is unknown. Since the integral on the right is a convolution integral, the convolution theorem provides a convenient formula for solving it. Laplace!

\[Y = F + YK\] \[Y=\frac{F}{1-K}\]

10.8 Example 2

TipExample

Solve

\[y(t)=1+2\int_0^t e^{-2(t-\tau)} y(\tau)\,d\tau.\]

CautionSolution

\[Y = \frac{1}{s}+2 \left(\frac{1}{s-^-2}\right)Y\] \[Y\left(1-\frac{2}{s+2}\right)=\frac{1}{s}\] \[Y\left(\frac{s+2}{s+2}-\frac{2}{s+2}\right)=\frac{1}{s}\]

\[Y=\frac{s+2}{s^2}\] \[Y=\frac{1}{s}+\frac{2}{s^2}\] \[\Rightarrow\]

\[y(t)=1+ 2t\]

10.9 Example 3

TipExample

Solve Integrodifferentiable equation

\[y'(t)=1+\int_0^t y(z)\,dz\qquad, y(0)=0\]

CautionSolution

Applying Laplace: \[sY=\frac{1}{s}+\mathscr{L}\left\{\int_0^t y(z)\,dz\right\}\] \[sY=\frac{1}{s}+\mathscr{L}\left\{\int_0^t (y(z)*1)\,dz\right\}\]

\[sY=\frac{1}{s}+\mathscr{L}\left\{\int_0^t (y(z)*(t-z)^0)\,dz\right\}\]

\[sY=\frac{1}{s}+\mathscr{L}\{y(t)\}\mathscr{L}\{t^0\}\] \[sY=\frac{1}{s}+Y\frac{1}{s}\] \[sY-Y\frac{1}{s}=\frac{1}{s}\] \[Y\left(\frac{s^2}{s}-\frac{1}{s}\right)=\frac{1}{s}\] \[Y=\frac{1}{s^2-1}\] \[\Rightarrow \boxed{y(t)=\sinh t}\,,\qquad t>1\]
