Preface to Unit 3:

Advanced Techniques in Solving Differential Equations (part II)

Unit 3 of our differential equations course ventures into the exploration of advanced techniques crucial for solving differential equations that are not easily tackled by the methods discussed in previous units. This unit is designed to equip students with the mathematical tools needed to address complex problems in engineering, physics, and applied mathematics. Each lecture delves into a specific topic, gradually building the foundation necessary for understanding and applying these advanced methods.

L18: Power Series (7.1)

This lecture introduces the concept of power series and their role in solving differential equations. We will cover the basics of power series, including their definition, convergence, and how they can be used as a tool to find solutions to differential equations near a point where the solution is analytic.

L19: Series Solutions I (7.2)

In the first part of series solutions, we focus on finding solutions to differential equations using power series methods. This lecture covers the technique of substituting a power series into a differential equation and determining the coefficients of the series to satisfy the equation.

L20: Series Solutions II (7.3)

Continuing from the previous lecture, we delve deeper into series solutions of differential equations. This session emphasizes solving more complex differential equations, including those with variable coefficients, using the power series approach.

L21: Intro to Laplace Transform, Part I (8.1)

The first part of our introduction to the Laplace Transform begins with the basic definitions and properties. We explore how the Laplace Transform can be used to transform a differential equation into an algebraic equation, simplifying the process of finding solutions.

L22: Intro to Laplace Transform, Part II (8.1)

Building on the foundations laid in the previous lecture, we continue to explore the Laplace Transform, focusing on its application to initial value problems. This lecture provides further insight into how the transform can be utilized to solve differential equations more efficiently.

L23: Inverse Laplace Transform (8.2)

This lecture introduces the concept of the Inverse Laplace Transform and its importance in solving differential equations. We discuss methods for finding the inverse transform and how it can be used to convert solutions back from the Laplace domain to the time domain.

L24: Laplace Transform and the Derivative (8.3)

We explore the relationship between the Laplace Transform and derivatives, demonstrating how the transform simplifies the process of dealing with differential equations involving derivatives. This lecture shows how to apply the transform to solve higher-order differential equations.

L25: Solving ODEs with Piecewise Continuous Forcing Functions (8.4)

This session focuses on solving ordinary differential equations (ODEs) that are subjected to piecewise continuous forcing functions. We discuss the handling of discontinuities and how the Laplace Transform can be used to manage such cases effectively.

L26: Periodic Forcing Functions (8.5)

We examine the application of the Laplace Transform in solving ODEs with periodic forcing functions. This lecture highlights the technique of using the Laplace Transform to simplify and solve differential equations driven by periodic inputs.

L27: Convolution Integral (8.6)

The Convolution Integral is introduced as a powerful tool in the context of the Laplace Transform. This lecture explains the concept of convolution, its properties, and how it can be used to find the solution to linear systems with arbitrary inputs.

L28: Impulse Forcing Functions (8.7)

In the final lecture of this unit, we focus on the response of systems to impulse forcing functions. We discuss the Dirac delta function and how it is used in conjunction with the Laplace Transform to solve differential equations that model physical systems subjected to sudden forces.

Each lecture in this unit is designed to build upon the last, ensuring students develop a comprehensive understanding of advanced methods in solving differential equations. Through detailed explanations, examples, and exercises, students will gain the skills necessary to tackle complex problems using these sophisticated mathematical tools.
