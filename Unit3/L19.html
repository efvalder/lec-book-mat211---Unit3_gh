2.1 Singular and Ordinary Points

NoteDefinition

For \[P_0(x)y''+P_1(x)y'+P_2(x)y=0\] where \(P_0,P_1,P_2\) are polynomial. The points \(x_0\) for which \(P_0(x_0)=0\) are called singular points and points \(x_0\) for which \(P_0(x_0)\neq 0\) are called ordinary points.

2.1.1 Example

TipExample
  1. Legendre’s Equation1 \[(1-x^2)y''-2xy'+\alpha(\alpha+1)y=0\]
  2. Besse’ls Equation2 \[x^2y''+xy'+(x^2-v^2)y=0\]
CautionSolution:
  1. \(x_0=\pm 1\)
  2. \(x_0=0\)

2.2 Theorem

Note

Theorem 2.1 (Unique Solution for not singular point.) Consider \[P_0(x)y''+P_1(x)y'+P_2(x)y=0\] \[ y(x_0)=a_0,\quad y'(x_0)=a_1\] If \(x_0\) is not singular point, then the equation has a unique solution on the largest open interval that contains \(x_0\) but not singular points equals to \[y=\sum_{n=0}^\infty a_n(x-x_0)^n\]

2.2.1 Example 1

TipExample

Find the power series solution for \[L_y:=y''+y=0,\quad\text{in terms of}(x-x_0)\]

CautionSolution

\[y=\sum_{n=0}^\infty a_n(x-x_0)^n\] \[y'=\sum_{n=1}^\infty na_n(x-x_0)^{n-1}\] \[y''=\sum_{n=2}^\infty n(n-1)a_n(x-x_0)^{n-2}\]

\[\Rightarrow\] \[L_y:=\sum_{n=2}^\infty n(n-1)a_n(x-x_0)^{n-2}+\sum_{n=0}^\infty a_n(x-x_0)^n=0\] Shifting index for \(y''\): \[=\sum_{n=2-2}^{\infty-2} (n+2)(n+2-1)a_{n+2}(x-x_0)^{n+2-2}+\sum_{n=0}^\infty a_n(x-x_0)^n\] \[=0\]

\[\sum_{n=0}^\infty \left[(n+2)(n+1)a_{n+2}+a_n\right](x-x_0)^n=0\]

\[\Leftrightarrow\]

\[(n+2)(n+1)a_{n+2}+a_n=0,\qquad \forall n\geq 0\]

\[\Leftrightarrow \boxed{a_{n+2}=-\frac{a_n}{(n+2)(n+1)}}\] (2\(^{nd}\) order recurrence relation)3 \[a_0,a_1,a_2,a_3,\cdots\] sequence depends upon both \(a_0,a_1\).

\(\boxed{\text{Case 1 (even index)}:}\)

\[ a_2=-\frac{a_0}{(0+2)(0+1)}=-\frac{a_0}{2\cdot 1}\] \[a_4=-\frac{a_2}{(2+2)(2+1)}=-\frac{a_2}{4\cdot 3}=\frac{a_0}{4\cdot3\cdot 2\cdot 1}\] \[a_6=-\frac{a_4}{(4+2)(4+1)}=-\frac{a_4}{6\cdot 5}=-\frac{a_0}{6!}\] Now instead of a recurrence relation, we can state an explicit equation for the even index:

\[\boxed{a_{2m}=(-1)^m\frac{a_0}{(2m)!},\qquad m=0,1,2,3,\cdots}\]4

\(\boxed{\text{Case 2 (odd index)}:}\)

\[a_3=-\frac{a_1}{(1+2)(1+1)}=-\frac{a_1}{3\cdot 2}\] \[a_5=-\frac{a_3}{(3+2)(3+1)}=\frac{a_1}{5\cdot 4\cdot 3\cdot 2}\] \[a_7=-\frac{a_5}{(5+2)(5+1)}=-\frac{a_1}{7!}\]

\[\boxed{a_{2m+1}=(-1)^m\frac{a_1}{(2m+1)!},\qquad m=0,1,2,3,\cdots}\]

Final Solution:

\[y=\sum_{m=0}^\infty (-1)^m a_0 \frac{(x-x_0)^{2m}}{(2m)!}+\sum_{m=0}^\infty (-1)^m a_1 \frac{(x-x_0)^{2m+1}}{(2m+1)!}\] \[y=a_0\sum_{m=0}^\infty (-1)^m \frac{(x-x_0)^{2m}}{(2m)!}+a_1\sum_{m=0}^\infty (-1)^m \frac{(x-x_0)^{2m+1}}{(2m+1)!}\]

Expanding cosine and sine function using taylor series:

\[\boxed{y=a_0\cos(x-x_0)+a_1\sin(x-x_0)}\]

Note: Check the Taylor series for cosine and sine functions5

2.2.2 Example 2

TipExample

Find the power series in \(x\) for the general solution of: \[L_y:=(1+2x^2)y''+6xy'+2y=0\]

CautionSolution

\[y=\sum_{n=0}^\infty a_nx^n\] \[y'=\sum_{n=1}^\infty na_nx^{n-1}\] \[y''=\sum_{n=2}^\infty n(n-1)a_nx^{n-2}\] \[L_y=(1+2x^2)\sum_{n=2}^\infty n(n-1)a_nx^{n-2}+6x\sum_{n=1}^\infty na_nx^{n-1}+2\sum_{n=0}^\infty a_nx^n=0\] \[=\sum_{n=2}^\infty n(n-1)a_nx^{n-2}+\sum_{n=0}^\infty[2n(n-1)+6n+2]a_nx^n\] \[=\sum_{n=2}^\infty n(n-1)a_nx^{n-2}+2\sum_{n=0}^\infty(n+1)^2a_nx^n\] \[=\sum_{n=0}^\infty (n+2)(n+1)a_{n+2}x^{n}+2\sum_{n=0}^\infty(n+1)^2a_nx^n\] \[=\sum_{n=0}^\infty \left[(n+2)(n+1)a_{n+2}+2(n+1)^2a_n\right]x^n=0\] \[\Leftrightarrow (n+2)(n+1)a_{n+2}+2(n+1)^2a_n=0,\quad\forall n\geq 0\]

\[\Rightarrow \boxed{a_{n+2}=-2\left(\frac{n+1}{n+2}\right)a_n}\] \(\boxed{\text{Case 1: Even terms: }}\)

\[a_2=-2\frac{1}{2}a_0=-\frac{1}{1}a_0\] \[a_4=-2\frac{3}{4}a_2=\frac{3\cdot 1}{2\cdot 1}a_0\]

\[a_6=-2\frac{5}{6}a_4=-\frac{5\cdot 3\cdot 1}{3\cdot 2\cdot 1}a_0\] \[a_{2m}=(-1)^m\frac{\Pi_{j=1}^m (2j-1)}{m!}a_0\]

\(\boxed{\text{Case 2: Odd terms: }}\)

\[a_3=-2\frac{(1+1)}{(1+2)}a_1=-\frac{4}{3}a_1\] \[a_5=-2\frac{(3+1)}{(3+2)}a_3=-2\frac{4}{5}a_3=2\frac{4}{5}\frac{4}{3}a_1=4^2\frac{2\cdot 1}{5\cdot 3}a_1\]

\[a_7=-2\frac{(5+1)}{(5+2)}a_5=-2\frac{6}{7}a_5=-4^3\frac{3\cdot 2\cdot 1}{7\cdot 5\cdot 3}a_1\]

\[a_{2m+1}=\frac{(-1)^m4^mm!}{\Pi_{j=1}^m(2j+1)}a_1\]

Solution:

\[\boxed{y=a_0\sum_{m=0}^\infty (-1)^m\frac{\Pi_{j=1}^m(2j-1)}{m!}x^{2m}+a_1\sum_{m=0}^\infty\frac{(-1)^m4^mm!}{\Pi_{j=1}^m(2j+1)}x^{2m+1}}\]

Note: Please observe that the solution is the addition of even and odd powers of \(x\), with different factors.
